    Kondapalli Bommalu

    DESCRIPTION:

    Kondapalli Bommalu is the name given to wooden toys that have been traditionally made for over 400 years by artists of Kondapalli, a small village near Vijayawada, Andhra Pradesh. The toys are made by Aryakhastriyas, who are said to have migrated from Rajasthan in the 16th Century, and claim their origin to Mukhtarishi who had the blessing of Lord Shiva.

    TYPES OF KONDAPALLI BOMMALU:

    Dasavatarams (ten incarnations of Lord Vishnu), elephants with ambari, and dancing dolls are among the most popular toys. Some Aryakhastriyas also make figures of mythology, Shri Krishna Geethopadesam, marriage rituals and replicas of palanquin-bearers carrying the bride and bridegroom. Sets of village craftsmen, bullock carts, and other scenes depicting the forgotten rural life such as milking the cow are also common.

    USES OF KONDAPALLI BOMMALU:

    The toys are great decoration pieces for living rooms and showcases, but are notably used for Bommalakoluvu during Sankranti and Navrathri. These can also be gifted on birthdays or at house warming parties.

    CHARACTERISTICS OF KONDAPALLI BOMMALU:

    Tella Poniki, a locally-available soft wood, is used for making these toys. After the wood has been carved out and the edges have been smooth finished, the toys are colored using oil or water colors or vegetable dyes. Enamel paints are also used depending upon the type of toys. No harmful chemicals are used and if you wish to buy these toys for your kids, go ahead, as these are perfectly safe.
